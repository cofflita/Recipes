Pisca Andina

The Pisca Andina, a simple yet flavorful soup, originates from the Venezuelan Andes. It’s made with basic ingredients: cilantro, green onions, water, diced potatoes, eggs, and milk. This comforting dish is commonly enjoyed during breakfast. Traditionally, it’s served alongside either wheat arepas or corn arepas. The star ingredient is the Andean potato, which thrives in this mountainous region.

Ingredients

  • 1 liter of concentrated chicken or hen broth
  • 4 large white potatoes, diced
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 1 finely chopped green onion (cebollín)
  • 3 cloves of garlic, well crushed
  • Salt and freshly ground pepper to taste
  • 4 eggs
  • 4 tablespoons of heavy cream
  • Cubes of white cheese (preferably smoked), to taste
  • Finely chopped cilantro, to taste

Preparation

  1. In a pot, combine the chicken broth and diced potatoes. Cook until the potatoes are tender.
  2. In a separate skillet, sauté the green onion and garlic in butter.
  3. Add the sautéed mixture to the potatoes and let the broth reduce.
  4. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Carefully add the eggs one by one and allow them to cook.
  6. Stir in the heavy cream and cheese until the cheese slightly melts. Be cautious not to let it boil.
  7. Remove from heat and add the finely chopped cilantro.
  8. Serve piping hot in preferably clay bowls to retain the warmth.
